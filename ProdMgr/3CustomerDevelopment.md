Exploratory Interview - free form, new ideas, pain points, open ended 
Validation interview - test your theory  - my solution solves their problem, - my feature is used for xx,

Satisfaction interview - what is good/bad
Efficiency interview - how to improve. how easy it is to use feature x, if you want to do x, what do you do?

Pre Product - new customers
Post Product - existing customers
Who to talk to ? 
  Write down 3 potential customer groups on X axis. 
  Market Size, Pain:Payment (would they pay for their pain) , Accessibility (how easily to contact these people) on  Y-axis. 
  Pick the one with max score.
Find interviewees (Pre Launch)- LinkedIn, Forums (Reddit, Quora), Twitter, Competitors (identify on social media pages/blogs)
Find interviewees (Post Launch) - not all users. identify users who have problems. 
    use live chat system logs to find users, active on your blog, power users (frequency of usage, buying, engagement etc), twitter
How to get them to talk? 
    Cold emails - Not very efficient. 
    But can be improved by Be short, Be personal (not autogenerated, ), make them feel valuable. not from sales, make them feel special
    Practice emails 
How to run Interview correctly - 
    talk about their problems and needs. they don't know what they want but know needs. Don't talk about solution. we need to understand why.
    don't give your opinions, comfortable environment, dont force conversation but just guide,
    what are their habits, why do they need our product, when do they need, where
    
   Ask Open ended questions
   Don't ask binary questions
   Don't ask hypothetical questions
   Don't ask leading questions
   Don't ask them questions that might make them lie
